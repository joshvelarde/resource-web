Nintendo Wi-Fi Connection

Nintendo Wi-Fi Connection (WFC) was Nintendo’s first major online multiplayer service, launching on November 14, 2005, for the Nintendo DS and Wii. Unlike competitors, it offered free online play but relied on a friend code system, making matchmaking less seamless. Despite this limitation, it supported popular titles like Mario Kart DS, Animal Crossing: Wild World, and Super Smash Bros. Brawl, expanding Nintendo’s online presence. The service was discontinued on May 20, 2014, effectively ending online functionality for hundreds of games.
