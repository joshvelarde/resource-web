Wii U

The Wii U, released on November 18, 2012 (North America), was Nintendo’s first HD home console and featured the GamePad, a controller with a built-in touchscreen for dual-screen gameplay. While it introduced innovative ideas, such as off-TV play and asymmetrical multiplayer, the system struggled with marketing confusion and a lack of third-party support. Despite critically acclaimed titles like Super Mario 3D World, Mario Kart 8, and The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild, it sold only 13.56 million units, making it one of Nintendo’s least successful consoles. The Wii U was discontinued in early 2017, shortly before the launch of its successor, the Nintendo Switch.
