Nintendo 3DS

The Nintendo 3DS was a handheld gaming console released on February 26, 2011 (Japan) / March 27, 2011 (North America), known for its glasses-free stereoscopic 3D display. As the successor to the Nintendo DS, it introduced improved hardware, StreetPass and SpotPass connectivity, and a growing library of games, including Pok√©mon X & Y, Animal Crossing: New Leaf, and Mario Kart 7. Despite a rough launch, a price drop and strong game support solidified its success. Nintendo officially discontinued the 3DS family on September 16, 2020, marking the end of its dedicated handheld consoles as the company shifted focus to the Nintendo Switch.
